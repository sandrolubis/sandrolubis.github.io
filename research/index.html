Research

Stratospheric Planetary Wave Reflection

My research on atmospheric dynamics combines theory, numerical simulations and observational analyses to try to improve our understanding of how the atmosphere works. Recently I have been interested in trying to extend zonal-mean theories to regional climates, which I believe is of more relevance for making accurate climate projections.

Impacts of Tropical Waves on Extreme Weather

Convectively coupled equatorial waves (CCEWs) have a strong influence on the variability of tropical weather and the climate system, so Dr. Lubis sought to determine how CCEWs affected tropical precipitation. He used long-term observation data from the Tropical Rainfall Measuring Mission satellite, which revealed that CCEWs resemble equatorially trapped shallow water wave structures. Though less dominant than other types of tropical oscillations, Dr. Lubis confirmed that CCEWs had a major impact on precipitation variability during boreal summers and winters, which helped validate the effect of equatorial waves on high-impact weather for forecasting models
